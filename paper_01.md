# Paper 01

## Title

Sign Language Interpretation Using Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence

## Authors

Fatma M. Najib

## Publication

Neural Computing and Applications (2025)

## Research Objective

The paper provides a comprehensive review of sign language recognition and interpretation systems using machine learning, image processing, artificial intelligence, and animation technologies.

The study aims to bridge the gap between research proposals and real-world applications for deaf and mute individuals.

## Key Areas Covered

1. Sign Language to Text/Speech Conversion
2. Speech/Text to Sign Language Conversion
3. Hand Gesture Recognition
4. Facial Expression Recognition
5. Lip Reading
6. Real-Time Sign Language Recognition
7. Mobile Applications for Deaf and Mute Users

## Important Findings

### Sign Language Recognition Pipeline

The paper describes a common workflow:

Input Images/Videos
→ Preprocessing
→ Hand and Face Tracking
→ Feature Extraction
→ Classification
→ Text Generation
→ Speech Generation

This workflow closely aligns with our ISL project pipeline.

### Popular Techniques Identified

* CNN (Convolutional Neural Networks)
* LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory)
* GRU
* Hidden Markov Models (HMM)
* MediaPipe
* OpenCV
* Deep Neural Networks

### Real-Time Recognition

The paper highlights MediaPipe and OpenCV as commonly used tools for real-time sign language recognition systems.

### ISL-Related Research

Several Indian Sign Language studies achieved strong performance using CNN, LSTM, and GRU architectures.

## Relevance to Our Project

This paper helped us:

* Understand the overall ISL recognition pipeline.
* Identify commonly used models.
* Explore real-time recognition approaches.
* Understand preprocessing requirements.
* Learn about future extensions such as text-to-speech and speech-to-sign conversion.

## Key Learning

A successful ISL system requires not only gesture recognition but also preprocessing, feature extraction, model training, and real-time deployment considerations.

## Limitations

This paper is a review paper and does not propose a single new model. Instead, it summarizes existing research and compares different approaches.

## How It Influenced Our Project

The paper helped us define the project workflow and guided our research into preprocessing techniques, model selection, and real-time implementation strategies.
